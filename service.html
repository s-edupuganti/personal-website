Cleve J. Fredricksen Library

I volunteered at the library from 2014 to 2019, where I was heavily involved with putting books back, as well as cleaning around the shelves of the library. It may not seem like it, but volunteering here has made me into the person I am today. I would spend countless hours being lost within books. Doing this service, also helped me navigate around a library really well, as I was originally extremely clueless before. Volunteering at the library taught me many qualities, and has also helped me become more selfless, and much more extroverted due to the fact that I had to help many people find books. Peer Teacher @ Texas A&M University

I am currently working as a peer teacher for ENGR 102, where I am happy to teach students about Python, as well as encourage them to continue programming during their time here at Texas A&M. A great deal of the students have not worked with programming before, so I would like to help these students. Essentially, my job is to guide students with labwork, homework, and any other concepts of Python. This acts as a great introduction to engineering, because there are many fields within A&M engineering that utilizes Python.

I enjoy doing this work because allows me to give students the mentorship, that I felt that I did not have from my TA's.
